1. Getting Started

1.1 Creating an account

Open the app, tap Sign Up, enter your email, a password, and a display name. We send a verification email immediately — check your inbox (and Spam folder). You must verify your email before prayer reminders can be scheduled.

1.2 Email verification didn't arrive

  • Wait 60 seconds, then check your Spam or Promotions folder.
  • Tap Resend verification email on the sign-in screen.
  • Confirm you typed your email correctly — typos are the #1 cause.
  • Still nothing after 10 minutes? Email us and we'll verify your account manually.

1.3 Allowing location and notifications

On first launch, Salah Deen asks for two permissions:

  • Location — required to calculate accurate prayer times and Qibla direction for your exact position. Without this, the app cannot work.
  • Notifications — required to send prayer reminders and the adhan alarm. Without this, you'll need to open the app to check times.

If you accidentally denied either, you can re-enable them in your device's Settings app. See Troubleshooting below.

2. Prayer Times

2.1 Times look wrong or are for a different city

  • Make sure location permission is set to Always or While Using.
  • Pull down on the home screen to refresh.
  • Check your Madhab setting (Settings → Madhab). Hanafi Asr is calculated differently from Shafi'i/Maliki/Hanbali Asr; a wrong madhab gives a wrong Asr by 30–60 minutes.
  • If you recently traveled, force-close and re-open the app to trigger a fresh location lookup.

2.2 Times haven't updated for the new day

Salah Deen caches the next 7 days of prayer times locally so the app works offline. If you've been offline longer than that, connect to the internet and re-open the app — times will refresh automatically. The app re-fetches whenever you move more than ~2.5 km.

2.3 Which calculation method does Salah Deen use?

Prayer times are calculated using the Aladhan API, a widely respected open prayer-time service. The default method follows the most common convention for your region; your selected Madhab adjusts the Asr calculation only.

3. Prayer Notifications & Adhan Alarm

3.1 I'm not getting any prayer notifications

  1. Confirm OS notification permission is granted: Settings → Salah Deen → Notifications → Allow Notifications.
  2. Inside the app: Settings → Prayer Reminders — make sure the master toggle and the individual prayer toggles are on.
  3. Android only: disable battery optimization for Salah Deen (Settings → Apps → Salah Deen → Battery → Unrestricted). Aggressive battery savers can kill scheduled alarms.
  4. iOS only: check Focus modes — if a Focus is on, prayer alerts may be silenced. Add Salah Deen to the Focus's allowed apps.
  5. Sign out and sign back in to refresh the push notification token.

3.2 The adhan alarm fired silently / I slept through Fajr

  • Your device may have been on Silent or Do Not Disturb. The adhan alarm respects the ringer switch by default.
  • Increase the alert sound level in Settings → Prayer Reminders → Alert Style.
  • On iOS, allow Critical Alerts for Salah Deen so Fajr can break through Do Not Disturb (an OS-level permission you grant once).
  • Make sure your phone isn't connected to a Bluetooth speaker that's powered off — audio routes there silently.

3.3 How far in advance can I be reminded?

For each prayer you can choose a pre-alert from 0 to 60 minutes before the time in Settings → Prayer Reminders. The adhan itself fires at the prayer time. Reminders are pre-scheduled for the next 7 days, so they work even if your phone is offline overnight.

4. Qibla Compass

4.1 The compass needle spins or points the wrong way

Your device compass needs to be calibrated:

  • Hold your phone flat and move it in a figure-8 motion a few times — this is the standard compass calibration motion on both iOS and Android.
  • Move away from metal objects, magnets, speakers, laptops, and car dashboards — they distort the magnetic field.
  • Restart the app after calibrating.

4.2 Does Qibla work indoors?

Yes — the Qibla bearing is calculated from your GPS coordinates (which work indoors if you have any signal), and the compass uses your device's magnetometer. Just be aware that indoor environments often have magnetic interference, so calibrate often.

5. Streaks & Prayer Logging

5.1 How is a prayer counted as on-time, late, or missed?

Status How it's set
On time Marked complete before the next prayer's start time
Late Marked complete after the next prayer began but before the day ends
Missed Not marked complete by the end of the day
Unmarked Default state until you tap to log

5.2 My streak reset and I think it's a bug

A streak continues as long as you log Fajr each day. If Fajr is logged as missed (or not logged by the end of the day), the streak resets. Streaks cannot be manually restored — this is intentional to keep accountability honest. If you believe there is a real data bug (e.g., your prayer was logged but the streak still reset), email us with your account email and the date in question.

5.3 Can I edit yesterday's prayer log?

You can mark a missed prayer for the current day until midnight local time. Past days are locked to preserve the integrity of streaks and group accountability.

6. Accountability Groups

6.1 Creating a group

Tap Groups → Create Group, give it a name, and you'll receive a 6-character invite code to share with friends or family. There is no limit on how many groups you can create or join.

6.2 Joining a group

Ask the group creator for the 6-character invite code, then tap Groups → Join Group and enter it.

6.3 What do other group members see about me?

  • Your display name.
  • Today's prayer statuses (on-time / late / missed / unmarked) for each of the five prayers.
  • Your current streak.

Other members cannot see your email, your location, your past days, or your AI coach feedback.

6.4 Leaving a group

Tap the group, then Leave Group. Your data is removed from that group immediately. If you were the last member, the group is deleted entirely.

7. AI Coach Feedback

The AI coach reviews your weekly aggregate prayer statuses (not individual prayers, not your location, not your group data) and generates a short, personalised piece of feedback to help you improve. New feedback is generated approximately once per week. If feedback feels off, you can safely ignore it — your streak and logs are unaffected.
